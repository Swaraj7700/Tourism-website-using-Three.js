HISTORY

The Jagannath Puri Temple, also known as the Shri Mandir, is one of the most revered Hindu temples located in Puri, Odisha, India. It is dedicated to Lord Jagannath, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu, along with his siblings, Lord Balabhadra and Devi Subhadra. The temple is considered one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites for Hindus.

The history of the Jagannath Puri Temple dates back to ancient times. The original temple is believed to have been built in the 12th century by King Anantavarman Chodaganga Deva of the Eastern Ganga dynasty. Over the centuries, the temple has undergone several renovations and additions by different rulers and devotees.

The temple complex covers a vast area and is enclosed by a high wall. The main temple structure, known as the "Deula," is a towering monument made of dark-colored stone. The Deula's architectural style is known as the Kalinga style, characterized by its pyramid-shaped roof and intricate carvings.

Transportation

Transportation to Jagannath Puri Temple is accessible through multiple modes. The nearest airport is Biju Patnaik International Airport in Bhubaneswar, approximately 60 kilometers away, from where you can hire a taxi or take a bus. Puri also has its own railway station, well-connected to major cities, and from there, auto-rickshaws or cycle rickshaws can be hired.

Accomodation

There are various accommodation options available near the Jagannath Puri Temple for visitors and pilgrims. These include hotels, guesthouses, dharamshalas, and ashrams. Visitors can choose from a range of budget-friendly to luxury accommodations based on their preferences and requirements. It is advisable to book your accommodation in advance, especially during peak pilgrimage seasons, to ensure a comfortable stay.

Review

The Jagannath Puri Temple is a divine abode that emanates spiritual energy, captivating devotees with its ancient history, awe-inspiring architecture, and an enchanting atmosphere of devotion and piety.
