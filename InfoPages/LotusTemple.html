HISTORY

The Lotus Temple, officially known as the Bahá'í House of Worship, is a magnificent architectural marvel located in Delhi, India. Its history traces back to the mid-20th century when the Bahá'í community decided to establish a place of worship that would embody the principles of unity, harmony, and equality. The temple was designed by Iranian-Canadian architect Fariborz Sahba and took nearly a decade to complete.

Construction of the Lotus Temple began in 1980, and it was finally inaugurated in 1986. The temple's unique design resembles a lotus flower, which is a symbol of purity and divinity in many cultures. It is composed of 27 free-standing marble-clad "petals" arranged in clusters, forming nine sides. The temple stands on a sprawling landscape adorned with lush gardens and reflective pools, creating a serene and tranquil ambiance.

The Lotus Temple's significance lies in its inclusivity and openness to people of all religions and backgrounds. It serves as a gathering place for people seeking spiritual solace, regardless of their faith. Visitors are invited to meditate, pray, or simply reflect in the tranquil environment of the temple. Today, the Lotus Temple has become a prominent landmark in Delhi, attracting millions of visitors from around the world who are captivated by its unique architecture and its message of unity and peace.

Transportation

The Lotus Temple, located in New Delhi, India, is easily accessible by various modes of transportation. If you're arriving by air, the nearest airport is the Indira Gandhi International Airport, from where you can hire a taxi or take a pre-paid cab to reach the temple, which is approximately 17 kilometers away. For those traveling by train, the nearest railway station is the Hazrat Nizamuddin Railway Station.

Accomodation

There are various accommodation options available near the Lotus Temple in Delhi, catering to the needs of visitors. From luxury hotels and boutique guesthouses to budget-friendly lodges and hostels, there are plenty of choices for travelers. Many of these accommodations offer comfortable rooms, modern amenities, and convenient access to the Lotus Temple.

Review

The Lotus Temple in Delhi is a mesmerizing architectural marvel that embodies serenity and spiritual harmony, offering a tranquil escape from the bustling city, and leaving visitors in awe of its graceful lotus-inspired design and serene ambiance.
