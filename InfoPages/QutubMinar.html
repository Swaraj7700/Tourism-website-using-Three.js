HISTORY

The Qutub Minar is a towering masterpiece of Islamic architecture located in Delhi, India. Its history dates back to the early 13th century when it was commissioned by Qutub-ud-Din Aibak, the founder of the Delhi Sultanate. The construction of the minar began in 1192, and it was completed by Qutub-ud-Din Aibak's successor, Iltutmish, in 1220.

The Qutub Minar stands as a symbol of victory and power. It was built to commemorate the triumph of Muslim rule over the last Hindu kingdom in Delhi. The minar is a five-storeyed structure, reaching a height of approximately 73 meters (240 feet). Each storey is beautifully adorned with intricate carvings and inscriptions from the Quran, making it a remarkable example of Indo-Islamic architecture.

Over the centuries, the Qutub Minar has faced various challenges, including earthquakes and lightning strikes, which resulted in some damage. However, it has been restored and preserved by different rulers and conservation efforts. Today, the Qutub Minar is not only a UNESCO World Heritage Site but also a popular tourist attraction, drawing visitors from around the world who admire its architectural grandeur and historical significance.

Transportation

Transportation to Qutub Minar, located in Delhi, can be easily arranged through various modes of transportation. The monument is well-connected to the city's transportation network. One can reach Qutub Minar by metro, with the nearest metro station being Qutub Minar Metro Station on the Yellow Line. From there, it's a short walk to the monument.

Accomodation

There are several accommodation options available near the Qutub Minar in Delhi to cater to the diverse needs of visitors. From luxury hotels and boutique accommodations to budget-friendly guesthouses and hostels, travelers have a range of choices. Many hotels in the vicinity offer comfortable rooms, modern amenities, and convenient access to the Qutub Minar.

Review

The Qutub Minar stands tall as a mesmerizing testament to India's rich history and architectural brilliance, captivating visitors with its intricate carvings and soaring height, making it an absolute must-see marvel in Delhi.
